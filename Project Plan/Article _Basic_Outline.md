# Article Basic Outline  
*(Architect’s Appendix Reference)*

Each WCB article follows this standard progression.  
Definitive sections establish *what* the subject is; descriptive sections explain *how* it behaves or connects.

---

### Standard Order

1. **Title and Header Block (YAML)**  
   - Required metadata block at the top of every file.

2. **Abstract or Introduction**  
   - One–three paragraphs summarizing purpose and scope.

3. **Contents List** *(optional but recommended for long articles)*  
   - Bulleted list of major sections for easy navigation.

4. **Explanatory Sections**  
   - Core conceptual discussion; defines key ideas.

5. **Mathematical or Structural Section** *(if applicable)*  
   - Formal relationships, equations, or schematics supporting the text.

6. **Applications or Examples** *(if applicable)*  
   - Practical demonstrations, model cases, or sample calculations.

7. **Terminology / Lexicon Section**  
   - Definitions of new or specialized terms introduced in the article.

8. **Crosslinks / Internal References Section**  
   - Links to directly related WCB articles.

9. **Version / Contributor Notes**  
   - Optional editorial or attribution block.

10. **External References Section** *(if applicable)*  
    - Sources of data, inspiration, or verification outside the Canon.
