# Mononics

**Mononics** is the discipline within the WCB framework concerned with the **structure, coherence, and persistence of worlds** — the study of how discrete, self-coherent bodies (*monons*) form, organize, and endure across all scales of matter and energy.  

Where **Morphotics** describes a world’s **form**, and **Animotics** its **breath**,  
**Mononics** describes its **body** — the substance of being and cohesion.

## Basic Framework

The hierarchical structure of Mononics follows the pattern:

- Parameters

This framework defines material existence across nested scales —  from its binding domain (*Class*),  
to its mass interval (*Series*),  structural phase (*Conformation*), and contextual organization (*System*).

### Detailed Framework

- **Mononic Classes**
  - **Duramons** — sub-stellar bodies  
    - *Planemons* — planetary-mass monons  
    - *Micromons* — asteroidal and small-body monons  
  - **Stellamons** — fusional, self-luminous bodies  
    - *Protostellamons* — forming or contracting stars  
    - *Main-Sequence Stellamons* — stable hydrogen-fusion stars  
    - *Poststellamons* — evolved or degenerate stars  
  - **Peramons** — trans-stellar and radiatively unstable bodies  
    - *Hyperstellamons* — extreme-mass fusion objects  
    - *Cryptomons* — collapse or exotic-state remnants  

## Systemic Framework

The Mononic hierarchy extends beyond individual bodies (*monons*) to describe  
how coherence manifests in larger, relational, and collective systems.

- **Monons** — the singular and self-coherent  
  - bound by *identity*  
- **Cules** — unity by *likeness* (homogeneous groups)  
  - e.g., duacules, multicules  
- **Lexes** — unity by *connection* (heterogeneous systems)  
  - e.g., stellalexes, duralexes  
- **Gates** — unity by *context* (collective or field aggregates)  
  - e.g., duagates, multigates, stellagates  

Each level marks a **mode of coherence**, not a scale of size —  
defining how monons combine to form higher structural orders.

# Conformics (material condition)

The study of **material regimes** and **structural phases** within monons.  
Defines how physical composition, bonding, and cohesion determine the *phase-state* of matter and its ability to sustain distinct morphologies.  
Bridges **Mononics** (structure) and **Morphotics** (form).

> **Primary conformic regimes:**  
> - **Lithic** — solid, rigid, or crystalline cohesion.  
> - **Rheic** — fluid, molten, or viscous cohesion.  
> - **Aeric** — gaseous, diffuse, or vaporous cohesion.  
> - **Ulsic** — energetic, plasma, or exotic-field cohesion.


# Animotics

**Animotics** is the discipline within the WCB framework concerned with the **origin, persistence, and diversity of vitality** — the study of how self-organizing, self-sustaining systems (*animota*) arise and endure across all chemistries, temperatures, and media.  

Where **Mononics** describes a world’s **body**, and **Morphotics** its **form**,  
**Animotics** describes its **breath** — the capacity for life.

## Basic Framework

The hierarchical structure of Animotics follows the pattern:

- **Order**
  - **Realm**
    - **Province**
      - **Environment**

This framework defines vitality across multiple scales — from its chemical foundation (*Order*),  
to its planetary equilibrium (*Realm*), regional expression (*Province*), and local adaptation (*Environment*).

### Detailed Framework

- **Biotic Order**
  - **Realms & Provinces**
    - **Geotic Realm**
      - *Gaean Province*
    - **Rheic Realm**
    - **Telluric Realm**

- **Exotic Order**
  - **Realms & Provinces**
    - **Xenotic Realm**
      - *Ammoniac Province*
      - *Silicate Province*
      - *Cryotic Province*
      - *Plasmic Province*

- **Incertic Order**
  - *No established Realms or Provinces*  
    (Represents latent, extinct, or unrealized vitality —  
    technically the **ananimotic** condition within Animotics.)
