# Precession
*Note that this is a measure of the time it takes the obliquity to complete one full rotational cycle.*

- **Precession Period** *($Ϣ$)* —
  The duration of one full cycle of the precession of the equinoxes.
 - NOTE that $Ϣ = 0$ if $\varepsilon = 0$

For Earth:

$$
Ϣ = 25{,}772
$$
# Converting between Tropical and Sidereal Years
$$
\begin{aligned}
\chi_{trop} &= \chi_{sid}\,\left(1-\dfrac{\chi_{sid}}{Ϣ}\right) \\[1em]
Ϣ &= \left(\dfrac{\chi_{sid}^2}{\chi_{sid} - \chi_{trop}}\right) \\[1em]
\chi_{sid} &≈ \chi_{trop}\,\left(1 + \dfrac{\chi_{trop}}{Ϣ}\right)
\end{aligned}
$$

Where:
- $\chi_{trop}$ — the **tropical chronum** (measured relative to the precessing equinox)
- $\chi_{sid}$ — the **sidereal chronum** (measured relative to the fixed stars)
- $Ϣ$ — the **precessional period**, the time required for one full rotation of the equinoctial reference.
